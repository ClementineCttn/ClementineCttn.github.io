Research Focus


I am interested in the patterns, causes and consequences of inequality in cities. In past research, I have used the framework of complex systems to analyse and model urban evolutions in various contexts and at various spatial and temporal scales (the post-Soviet space, France, London). Going forward, I have two directions of research: 1/ to analyse the dynamics of economic inequality in cities as part of larger (interurban) systems : are cities getting richer, poorer or more unequal at the same pace? in a uniform way or in certain neighbourhoods more than others? What is the impact of intra and interurban migrations of people on the urban environments of origin and destination? and 2/ to develop agent-based models of economic inequality in cities in order to experiment and compare policy scenarios of inequality reduction: are changes to the environments more effective on people than changes on people in specific environments?

Teaching Focus


As a teacher, trainer and supervisor, I am a strong advocate of critical thinking, collaborative and hands-on practice , as well as the use of open source data and softwares. I have mainly taught urban studies and methodology subjects , including urban inequality, urban modelling, statistical and spatial analysis, map-making. I have done so since 2012 in various contexts (university curriculum, summer schools, self-teaching groups) and at various institutions (Univ. Paris 1, UCL, Univ. of Shanghai, CNRS, TU-Delft).
